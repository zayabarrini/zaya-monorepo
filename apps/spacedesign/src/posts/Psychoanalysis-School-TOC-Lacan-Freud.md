# Important dates in the History of psychoanalysis

Here’s a timeline of **important dates in the history of psychoanalysis**, highlighting key publications, events, and figures:

---

### 🧠 **19th Century: Foundations**

- **1885–1886**:
  **Sigmund Freud** studies under **Jean-Martin Charcot** in Paris, observing hypnosis used to treat hysteria — pivotal for Freud’s future theories.

- **1889**:
  Freud visits the **Nancy School** (Bernheim, Liébeault), deepening his knowledge of suggestion and hysteria.

---

### 📚 **Turn of the Century: Birth of Psychoanalysis**

- **1895**:
  Freud and Josef Breuer publish **"Studies on Hysteria"** (_Studien über Hysterie_) — often considered the **birth of psychoanalysis**.

  - Introduces concepts like the **talking cure** and **catharsis**.

- **1900**:
  Freud publishes **"The Interpretation of Dreams"** (_Die Traumdeutung_), introducing the **unconscious**, **dream-work**, and **wish-fulfillment**.

- **1905**:
  Publication of **"Three Essays on the Theory of Sexuality"**, which lays the groundwork for the concept of **infantile sexuality** and the **Oedipus complex**.

---

### 🧑‍🏫 **Early 20th Century: Institutionalization**

- **1909**:
  Freud travels to the **United States** (Clark University, Massachusetts) with Carl Jung, introducing psychoanalysis to an American audience.

- **1910**:
  Founding of the **International Psychoanalytical Association (IPA)** in Nuremberg, with **Carl Jung** as the first president.

- **1913**:
  Freud and Jung **split** due to theoretical disagreements (Jung’s rejection of the primacy of sexuality and the unconscious as Freud conceived it).

---

### 🔥 **World Wars and Theoretical Developments**

- **1920**:
  Freud publishes **"Beyond the Pleasure Principle"**, introducing the **death drive (Thanatos)**.

- **1923**:
  Freud’s **second topography**: id, ego, and superego introduced in **"The Ego and the Id"**.

- **1936**:
  **Lacan** presents his paper **"The Mirror Stage"**, marking the beginning of his influence on psychoanalysis.

- **1938**:
  Freud flees Austria due to the Nazi regime and settles in London, where he dies a year later.

---

### 🌀 **Mid–20th Century: Structural and Linguistic Turn**

- **1953**:
  **Jacques Lacan** begins his **seminars** in Paris, reinterpreting Freud through **structural linguistics** and **topology**.

- **1957**:
  Lacan formalizes the concept of **forclusion (foreclosure)** as the **mechanism of psychosis**.

- **1964**:
  Lacan delivers the **Seminar XI**: _"The Four Fundamental Concepts of Psychoanalysis"_, a key text for understanding his teaching.

---

### 🌍 **Late 20th Century: Expansion and Critique**

- **1970s**:
  Rise of **feminist critiques** of psychoanalysis (e.g., Julia Kristeva, Luce Irigaray, Hélène Cixous) and integration with **poststructuralism**.

- **1981**:
  **Lacan dies** in Paris. His legacy is carried forward by followers such as Jacques-Alain Miller and Éric Laurent.

- **1980s–2000s**:
  Expansion of psychoanalysis into **Latin America**, **Asia**, and **Africa**, and its **interdisciplinary applications** (film, philosophy, gender theory).

---

### 🧩 **21st Century: Contemporary Issues**

- Ongoing dialogues between psychoanalysis and:

  - **Neuroscience**
  - **Queer and trans theory**
  - **Trauma studies**
  - **Autism and psychosis** (especially in Lacanian circles)

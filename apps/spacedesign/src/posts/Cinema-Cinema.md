# Theater is composed of several elements, including:...

Theater is composed of several elements, including:

Theatrical Text: The written script that guides the play.
Scenography: The design of the scenery, which includes elements such as sets, props and costumes.
Acting: The interpretation of the characters by the actors.
Direction: The director's guidance and supervision over the production.
Lighting: The use of light to create atmosphere and highlight specific elements.
Sound Design: The use of sounds, music and sound effects.

# Makeup: The application of makeup to characterize the characters....

Makeup: The application of makeup to characterize the characters.
Stage Space: The place where the play is performed, which can be a theater, stage or alternative space. Audience: The audience that attends the performance.
Time: The length of the play and the organization of time within the narrative.

# Elements of Cinema

Script .: The script is the basis of film production, containing the narrative, dialogue, and directions for the technical team and actors.Direction: The director's vision and guidance are fundamental to the interpretation of the story, choice of scenes, direction of actors, and the overall tone of the film.Photography .Cinematography .: The way the film is visually captured, including camera choices, lighting, composition, and color.Sound: Includes soundtrack, sound effects, dialogue, and sound mixing to create an immersive listening experience.Editing: The assembly and organization of scenes to create the desired pace, continuity, and narrative.Acting: The performance of the actors, which brings the characters to life and contributes to the emotional authenticity of the story.Set Design: The production design, including sets, costumes, and props, that creates the visual world of the film.

Visual Effects .VFX. : Technology and techniques for creating visual elements that cannot be captured during filming, such as special effects. Soundtrack : The original or selected music that accompanies the film, adding emotion, atmosphere, and complementing the narrative. Sound Editing : The manipulation and mixing of sound elements to create special effects and enhance the overall sound quality. Genre : The categorization of the film based on characteristics such as theme, style, and tone, such as drama, comedy, action, thriller, among others. Camera and Camera Movement : The choice of camera types, lenses, and camera movements that contribute to the visual aesthetic and narrative of the film.

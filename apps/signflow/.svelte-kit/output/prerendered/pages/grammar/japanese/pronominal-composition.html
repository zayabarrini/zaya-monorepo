Note on Japanese Pronominal Composition

In Japanese, unstressed object pronouns are typically incorporated into verb morphology through specific grammatical constructions rather than as separate words. These include:

  • て-form + くれる/あげる/もらう - indicating giving/receiving actions
  • Causative-passive forms - indicating actions done for/by someone
  • Honorific/humble forms - indicating social relationships

The color-coded pronouns in the examples above represent these incorporated pronominal elements in verb conjugations.

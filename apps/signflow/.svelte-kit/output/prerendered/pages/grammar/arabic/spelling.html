Arabic Writing System Basics

1
Right-to-Left Writing

Arabic is written from right to left, which is the opposite direction of English and most European languages.

اللغة العربية تُكتب من اليمين إلى اليسار
The Arabic language is written from right to left
Important: Numbers in Arabic are written from left to right, even within Arabic text!
2
Cursive Script

Arabic letters connect to each other (with some exceptions) within words. Most letters have 4 forms: isolated, initial, medial, and final.

كتاب (kitaab) - book
kāf (initial) + tā (medial) + ʾalif (medial) + bā (final)
Notice how all letters connect in the word "book"
3
No Capital Letters

Arabic doesn't have capital letters. Proper nouns are distinguished by context, not letter case.

محمد ذهب إلى القاهرة
Muhammad dhahaba ilā al-Qāhira
Muhammad went to Cairo (note: no capitalization for names or places)

Essential Spelling Rules

1
Definite Article ال (al-) Rules

The definite article "al-" (ال) assimilates with "sun letters" (الحروف الشمسية).

Sun Letters (14)ExampleMoon Letters (14)Example
ت ث د ذ ر ز س ش ص ض ط ظ ل نالشمس (ash-shams)أ ب ج ح خ ع غ ف ق ك م ه و يالقمر (al-qamar)
النَّجْم (an-najm) - the star
ن is a sun letter, so الـ becomes النَّـ
The "l" of الـ assimilates with ن
2
Tāʾ Marbūṭah (ة) Rules

The feminine ending تاء مربوطة changes pronunciation and spelling based on its position in the sentence.

مُدَرِّسَةٌ (mudarrisatun)
Pronounced "t" when followed by case ending
هذه مُدَرِّسَة (hādhihi mudarrisah)
Pronounced "h" when at end of sentence
A female teacher
3
Shaddah (ّ) Doubling Rule

The shaddah indicates consonant doubling. The consonant is pronounced twice but written once with a shaddah mark.

كُتُب (kutub) vs. كُتَّب (kuttab)
/ku.tub/ (books) vs /kut.tab/ (writers)
The shaddah on ت changes meaning completely
4
Alif Maqṣūrah (ى) vs. Yāʾ (ي)

Alif maqṣūrah is written like ي but without dots. It only appears at the end of words and represents a long /aː/ sound.

على (ʻalā) - on/upon
Ends with alif maqṣūrah (ى), pronounced /aː/
علي (ʻalī) - Ali (name)
Ends with yāʾ (ي), pronounced /iː/
Different letters, different meanings

Hamza (ء) Spelling Rules

1
Hamza Carriers

Hamza must sit on a "chair" (carrier). The carrier is determined by the surrounding vowels.

PositionCarrierExampleRule
Initialأ إ آأكل (akala)On alif with matching harakah
Medialئ ؤسؤال (suʼāl)On yāʾ or wāw based on vowel strength
Finalءبناء (bināʼ)Independent or on alif if preceded by kasrah
2
Vowel Strength Hierarchy

Hamza carriers follow this vowel strength order: kasrah (ـِ) > ḍammah (ـُ) > fatḥah (ـَ)

سُئِلَ (suʼila) - he was asked
Hamza has kasrah, so it sits on yāʾ (ئ)
يَسْأَلُ (yasʼalu) - he asks
Hamza has fatḥah, so it sits on alif (أ)
The hamza carrier changes based on the vowel
3
Hamza al-Waṣl (همزة الوصل)

The connecting hamza appears at the beginning of words and is only pronounced when starting speech.

اسم (ism) - name
When isolated: /ism/
بسم الله (bismi-llāh)
In context: /bismillāh/ (hamza not pronounced)
The hamza disappears in connected speech

Common Spelling Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake 1: Confusing ت and ط

Both make a "t" sound, but ط is emphatic (pharyngealized).

تَين (incorrect for "figs")
طِين (correct - clay) vs تِين (correct - figs)

Mistake 2: Mixing up ذ, ز, and ظ

ذ is /ð/ (like "the"), ز is /z/, ظ is emphatic /ðˤ/.

هذا (hādhā) spelled as هزا
هَذا (this) vs هَزَ (he shook)

Mistake 3: Wrong Hamza Placement

Placing hamza on wrong carrier based on vowel.

سؤال spelled as سؤال (hamza should be on wāw)
سُؤال (question) - hamza on wāw because preceding vowel is ḍammah

Mistake 4: Forgetting Shaddah

Omitting shaddah changes meaning completely.

علم (ʻilim) for knowledge vs
عَلِمَ (ʻalima) he knew vs عَلَّمَ (ʻallama) he taught

Quick Tips for Better Spelling

📚
Read Extensively

The more you read Arabic, the more you internalize correct spelling patterns naturally.

✍️
Practice Writing

Regular writing practice helps muscle memory. Try copying short texts word-for-word.

🎯
Learn Root Patterns

Arabic words derive from 3-letter roots. Recognizing roots helps with spelling derivatives.

🔍
Use a Dictionary

When in doubt, check a reliable Arabic dictionary for correct spelling and diacritics.

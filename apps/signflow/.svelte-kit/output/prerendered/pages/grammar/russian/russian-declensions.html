Key Points about Russian Declensions

1. Cases indicate grammatical function:

Nominative: Subject of the sentence (who/what is doing the action)

Genitive: Possession, absence, after certain prepositions and numbers

Dative: Indirect object (to/for whom), after certain verbs

Accusative: Direct object (whom/what the action affects), after certain prepositions

Instrumental: Means by which action is done (with what), after certain prepositions

Prepositional: Always used with prepositions (about, in, on)

2. Gender matters: Masculine, feminine, and neuter nouns have different declension patterns.

3. Animacy affects accusative: For masculine animate nouns, the accusative form is the same as genitive, not nominative.

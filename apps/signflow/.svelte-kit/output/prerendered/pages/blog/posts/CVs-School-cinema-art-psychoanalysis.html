Admission Conditions and Affirmative Policies

The Scholarship and Grants Committee of the Forum of the Lacanian Field of Rio de Janeiro was established in July 2021 in a format inspired by Cartel and is working on formulating an Affirmative Policies Project to democratize access and training of new analysts aimed at low-income professionals, primarily Afro-descendants and indigenous peoples. The selection criteria will be subject to specific internal regulations and will follow specific guidelines through a public notice, formulated by this Committee. The objective is to implement a policy of admission to training that supports the essential proportionality of the members and participants of FCL RJ with the ethnic composition of the Brazilian population.

Psychoanalysis in China + Chinese language - Fortnightly - Thursdays 7pm

He is one of the founders of the Beijing Psychoanalysis Institute and the author of works on psychoanalysis. 2. ..Shen Heyong..: He is another prominent Chinese psychoanalyst who contributed to the dissemination of psychoanalysis in China. He is also one of the founders of the Beijing Psychoanalytic Institute. 3. ..Xiaowei Jiang..: She is a Chinese psychoanalyst who has written extensively on psychoanalysis and psychological issues. She is known for her contributions to the understanding of psychoanalysis in China. 4. ..Yang Yanning..: Yang Yanning is a psychoanalyst who teaches and practices psychoanalysis in China. He is also the author of works that explore the clinical applications of psychoanalysis in contemporary Chinese society.

Bibliography

“Psychoanalysis in China: Contemporary Growth” .2012. by Shen Heyong and John M. R. Hughes: This book explores the development of psychoanalysis in China and its clinical applications, as well as the interactions between psychoanalytic theory and Chinese culture. “The View from Below” .2009. by Xiaowei Jiang: This book offers a look at psychoanalysis in China, addressing issues such as sexuality, family, and Chinese culture through a psychoanalytic lens. “Psychoanalysis: An East-West Dialogue”.2016. by Xiaowei Jiang and Henderikus J. Stam: This book examines the dialogue between Western psychoanalysis and Chinese culture, exploring the application and adaptation of psychoanalysis in Chinese contexts. “The Wound and the Bond: An Introduction to Chinese Psychoanalysis”.2016. by Yu Xin: This book explores Chinese psychoanalysis and its challenges in understanding the Chinese psyche, focusing on issues such as family and identity.

Bibliography

Psychopathology of Pre-Islamic Arabia. by Rachid Aouameur: This book examines psychopathology in pre-Islamic Arabian society. “Le temps de l’adieu: Une psychopathologie du ‘déjà-vu’“.The Time of Farewell: A Psychopathology of ‘I’ve Seen That Before’. by Rachid Aouameur: In this book, the author explores the notion of “déjà-vu” and its importance in psychopathology.

Bibliography

Russian psychoanalytic literature has a rich history, with several notable Russian psychoanalysts having written important books and works over the years. Below are some of the most influential books written by Russian psychoanalysts: “Destruction as the End of Development” .1923. by Sabina Spielrein: In this book, Sabina Spielrein explores her ideas about aggression and destruction, contributing to the understanding of the relationship between violence and human development. “The Dialectics of Loneliness: Lectures on Psychotherapy” .1921. by Lev Vygotsky : Although Lev Vygotsky is best known for his work in cultural psychology and sociocultural theory, this book contains essays on psychotherapy and offers valuable insights into the psychology of personality. “Psychoanalysis and the Passion for the Russian Soul” .1997. by Marina Bykova : In this book, Russian psychoanalyst Marina Bykova examines the intersection of psychoanalysis and Russian culture, exploring the peculiarities of Russian psychology and the cultural influences on psychoanalytic practice. ” Recollections of Sabina Spielrein: Writings on Psychoanalysis ” .1996. edited by Bernard Minder and Alain Ferrant: This book brings together writings by Sabina Spielrein , including correspondence with Sigmund Freud and Carl Gustav Jung, providing valuable insight into the contributions of this pioneering Russian psychoanalyst.

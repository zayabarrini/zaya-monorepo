Example Sentences with Color-Coded Syntax

الكتابُ جديدٌ وَالقلمُ قديمٌ
Translation: The book is new and the pen is old.
Explanation: Both nouns are definite (with الـ) and in nominative case (ُ). The adjectives agree in definiteness, gender, number, and case.
قرأتُ كتابي الجديد في المكتبة
Translation: I read my new book in the library.
Explanation: "كتابي" (my book) shows attached pronoun "ي". "الجديد" is definite adjective agreeing with the possessed noun. "المكتبة" is feminine noun with feminine marker "ة".
هذه سيارةٌ جميلةٌ وَتلك سيارةٌ قديمةٌ
Translation: This is a beautiful car and that is an old car.
Explanation: Demonstrative pronouns "هذه" (this) and "تلك" (that). Feminine nouns with "ة" marker. Adjectives agree in femininity with "ة" ending.
الولدانِ الصغيرانِ يلعبان في الحديقة
Translation: The two small boys are playing in the garden.
Explanation: Dual noun "الولدان" with dual marker "ان". Adjective matches with dual marker "ان". Verb "يلعبان" also has dual marker for agreement.

Key Rules of Arabic Noun & Adjective Conjugation:

  • Gender Agreement: Adjectives must match the gender of the noun they describe (masculine/feminine with "ة" marker).
  • Number Agreement: Adjectives match number (singular, dual with "ان/ين", plural).
  • Case Agreement: Both nouns and adjectives take case endings (nominative, genitive, accusative).
  • Definiteness Agreement: When a noun is definite (with الـ or attached pronoun), its adjective must also be definite.
  • Feminine Marker: Most feminine nouns and adjectives end with "ة" (ta marbuta).
  • Broken Plurals: Many Arabic nouns have irregular plural forms that change the internal structure of the word.
  • Sound Plurals: Regular masculine plural adds "ون/ين", feminine plural adds "ات".
  • Dual Forms: For two items, add "ان" for nominative, "ين" for genitive/accusative.

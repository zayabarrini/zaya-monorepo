Key Features of Arabic Syntax

  • VSO Word Order: Arabic typically follows Verb-Subject-Object order, but Subject-Verb-Object is also common, especially with definite subjects.
  • Case Endings: Arabic nouns and adjectives change their endings based on grammatical case (nominative, accusative, genitive).
  • Agreement: Verbs agree with their subjects in gender and number, and adjectives agree with the nouns they modify.
  • Pro-drop Language: Subject pronouns are often omitted since verb conjugation indicates the subject.
  • Flexible Word Order: Arabic allows some flexibility in word order for emphasis, with fronting of objects or adjuncts being common.
  • Idafa Construction: Possession is expressed through the إضافة construction (noun + noun without definite article).
  • Dual Forms: Arabic has special forms for dual (two of something) in addition to singular and plural.
  • Passive Voice: Formed by changing the vowel pattern of the verb (internal passive) or using prefix/suffix changes.

Rules of Pronominal Composition in Arabic

1 Direct Object Pronouns Come First

When both direct and indirect object pronouns attach to a verb, the direct object pronoun comes immediately after the verb root, followed by the indirect object pronoun.

2 Order Cannot Be Reversed

The sequence is fixed: Verb + Direct Object Pronoun + Indirect Object Pronoun. Reversing this order creates ungrammatical structures.

3 Phonetic Adjustments

When certain pronouns combine, phonetic adjustments occur (e.g., ه + ها → هاها becomes هَا with vowel lengthening).

4 Prepositional Pronouns as Indirect Objects

Indirect object pronouns often derive from prepositional pronouns (لـ + pronoun → له، لها، لنا etc.) but attach directly to the verb without the preposition.

Single Object Pronoun Examples

1
يعطيني الكتاب
Translation: He gives me the book. / He gives the book to me.
Analysis:
Direct Object: الكتاب (the book)
Indirect Object Pronoun: ـني (to me) attached to verb
يـعطيني = He-gives-me
Note: The indirect object pronoun ـني attaches directly to the verb, replacing the need for a separate prepositional phrase like "لي" (to me).
2
سأعطيه إياك
Translation: I will give it to you.
Analysis:
Direct Object Pronoun: ـه (it) attached to verb
Indirect Object: إياك (to you - separate word)
سأعطيه = I will-give-it
Note: When both objects are pronouns, sometimes one appears as a separate word (إياك) for clarity or emphasis.

Double Object Pronoun Attachments

3
أعطيتهاإياه
Translation: I gave it (feminine) to him.
Detailed Analysis:
Verb Structure: أعط (root: give) + ـيت (I - past tense suffix) = "I gave"
Direct Object Pronoun: ـها (it - feminine) attached to verb
Indirect Object Pronoun: إياه (to him) as separate word
أعطيت + ها + إياه = Gave-I + it(f) + to him
4
يعطيهك إياي
Translation: He gives it to you (and) to me.
Detailed Analysis:
Direct Object Pronoun: ـه (it) attached to verb
Indirect Object Pronoun: ـك (to you) attached to verb
Additional Indirect Object: إياي (to me) as separate word
يـعطيهك = He-gives-it-to you

Complex Examples with Analysis

5
لن يعطيكهإياها أبداً
Translation: He will never give it to you for her sake.
Complex Analysis:
Negation + Future: لن (will not) + يـ (he - present prefix)
Verb with Attached Pronouns: عطي (give) + ـك (to you) + ـه (it)
Benefactive Pronoun: إياها (for her - separate emphatic form)
Adverb: أبداً (never)
Structure: لن + يـعطي + كـ + هـ + إياها + أبداً
6
أعطتناكمإياهن بالأمس
Translation: We gave them (feminine) to you (plural) yesterday.
Complex Analysis:
Past Verb with Subject: أعط (give) + ـتنا (we - past suffix)
Indirect Object Pronoun: ـكم (to you plural) attached
Direct Object Pronoun: إياهن (them feminine) as separate word
Time Adjunct: بالأمس (yesterday)
Order: Verb + Indirect Object + Direct Object + Time

Key Points about Arabic Pronominal Composition

  • Direct object pronouns typically attach directly to the verb as suffixes when they're the only object.
  • When both direct and indirect object pronouns are present, the direct object attaches first, followed by the indirect object.
  • Some pronouns have two forms: attached (ـه، ـها، ـهم) and detached (إياه، إياها، إياهم) for emphasis or clarity.
  • In double pronoun constructions, sometimes one pronoun appears as a separate word (إياـ form) to avoid ambiguity.
  • The verb عطى (to give) is a classic example of a ditransitive verb that regularly takes both direct and indirect objects.
  • Phonetic assimilation can occur when certain pronouns combine, changing pronunciation but not meaning.
  • The order of pronouns is fixed: Verb + Direct Object Pronoun + Indirect Object Pronoun. This cannot be reversed.
  • When prepositions are involved, the prepositional phrase typically comes after the verb with attached pronouns.

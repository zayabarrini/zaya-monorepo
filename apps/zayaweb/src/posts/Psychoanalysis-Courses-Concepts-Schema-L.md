### The symbolic is where something missing can exist. There is the famous example of the library, taken...

The symbolic is where something missing can exist. There is the famous example of the library, taken by Lacan. In a library, the books are numbered. If someone takes one, there will be an empty space, a lack, because there is a symbolic order. Only in the symbolic order can something that is not there exist and be noticed — that book is missing. The most important thing for the librarian is not the books that one has, but the ones that are missing.

### The message leaves the subject and reaches the imaginary other, from the imaginary other I position ...

The message leaves the subject and reaches the imaginary other, from the imaginary other I position the self, but it continues in this arc that reaches the big Other. When the message reaches the big Other it undergoes a mutation, there is an inversion, the Other inverts the message and from this inverted place it is sent to the positionof the subject. - Summary of the L scheme is the subject receivingand its own message is inverted from the position of the Other, and this reception translates into the clinical operation called interpretation.

So we are pointing out here that instead of perfect communication, there is a kind of misunderstanding. ### Misunderstanding

### We understand each other, we are substitutable in relation to each other. I can take the other perso...

We understand each other, we are substitutable in relation to each other. I can take the other person's point of view, the other person can take my point of view. This whole relationship of understanding that Lacan criticizes is an imaginary relationship, because deep down, the other person is another person. You just put yourself in their place, you just think they are similar to you, but in fact, as we get closer, differences appear, and as we get further away, similarities appear, which are not perceptible up close.

### When I interpret, I am not understanding my patient, I am not making myself understood, I am not pro...

When I interpret, I am not understanding my patient, I am not making myself understood, I am not producing a meaning that would be the same for me and for him, but I am asserting a connected meaning, an ambiguous meaning, a meaning that this subject cannot recognize, precisely by using this position of the Other, crossing the imaginary axis of resistance and returning this message to the position of the subject.

Interpretation positions this subject and allows him to recognize the unconscious desire that was in his original message.

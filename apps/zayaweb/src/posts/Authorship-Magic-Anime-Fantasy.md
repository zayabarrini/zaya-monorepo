# Financial strategies for digital artists

As a digital artist, developing sound financial strategies can help ensure long-term success and sustainability. Here are some key financial strategies tailored for digital artists:

1. **Diversify Income Streams**

- **Freelance Work** : Offer freelance services in areas like character design, concept art, illustration, animation, or graphic design. Platforms like Upwork, Fiverr, or Behance can help you connect with clients.

- **Commissions** : Take on personal commissions from fans or customers, either through social media platforms (Instagram, Twitter, etc.) or your own website.

- **Prints and Merchandise** : Sell physical prints, posters, stickers, or even digital products like wallpapers or coloring pages on platforms like Etsy, Redbubble, or your own online store.

- **Online Courses and Tutorials** : Share your knowledge through paid courses on platforms like Skillshare, Udemy, or Gumroad. You can also offer paid tutorials or process breakdowns.

- **Patreon or Subscription Models** : Offer exclusive content like behind-the-scenes sketches, early access to new works, or personalized tutorials via Patreon or platforms like Ko-fi.

- **Stock Art and Licensing** : Sell your artwork as stock on platforms like Shutterstock, Adobe Stock, or Creative Market, or license your work to brands, agencies, or companies.

2. **Build a Financial Buffer**

- **Emergency Fund** : Since freelance work can be unpredictable, aim to build an emergency fund that covers 3-6 months of living expenses. This provides a cushion during slow periods or emergencies.

- **Savings Goals** : Set specific savings goals for both personal needs (retirement, healthcare) and professional needs (new equipment, software, education).

3. **Set Rates and Budget Wisely**

- **Know Your Value** : Research market rates for digital artists in your niche. Don’t undersell your work, and consider charging per project instead of per hour, which can account for creative time and revisions.

- **Factor in Overhead Costs** : Include the cost of software, hardware, taxes, and time spent on marketing, client communication, and revisions when calculating your rates.

- **Negotiate Contracts** : Always have contracts with clear terms about deadlines, payments, and revisions. This protects you from scope creep or delayed payments.

4. **Tax Planning and Financial Organization**

- **Track Income and Expenses** : Use accounting software like QuickBooks, FreshBooks, or even free tools like Wave to track your income, expenses, and profits.

- **Understand Taxes** : Research tax laws in your country and make sure to save for taxes if you’re a freelancer. Setting aside a percentage of your earnings (e.g., 20-30%) for taxes will help avoid surprises.

- **Write Off Business Expenses** : Keep track of deductible expenses such as software, hardware, website hosting, advertising, and education costs. These can lower your tax burden.

- **Hire a Tax Professional** : Consider hiring an accountant or financial advisor to help with tax filing and financial planning, especially as your income grows.

5. **Invest in Tools and Education**

- **Invest in Software and Hardware** : Regularly upgrade your tools (drawing tablets, computers, etc.) to stay competitive and improve efficiency. Don’t hesitate to buy industry-standard software or hardware if it enhances your workflow.

- **Education and Skill Growth** : Invest in courses, tutorials, or workshops to continue improving your skills and stay up to date with industry trends.

- **Social Media Marketing** : Build your audience on platforms like Instagram, Twitter, ArtStation, and TikTok. Regularly post updates, sketches, process videos, and interact with your audience to keep them engaged.

- **Portfolio Website** : Have a professional portfolio website that showcases your best work, client testimonials, and contact details. It serves as your digital business card.

- **Email Newsletter** : Build an email list to stay connected with your audience and clients. Offer updates, new product releases, or exclusive content to those subscribed.

7. **Passive Income and Long-Term Investments**

- **Create Digital Products** : Sell digital products like brushes, textures, templates, or design elements that can generate passive income over time.

- **Licensing and Royalties** : License your art for use in products, games, or media, and earn royalties. This can be a more stable income stream compared to freelance work.

- **Invest in Personal Finance** : Consider investing in stocks, bonds, or mutual funds to build long-term wealth. You can also look into retirement savings accounts (IRA, 401k) to ensure financial stability as you age.

8. **Collaboration and Networking**

- **Collaborate with Other Artists** : Partner with other artists or creators on joint projects, shared shops, or collaborations that could expand your audience and income opportunities.

- **Participate in Exhibitions and Conventions** : Attend conventions, art fairs, or exhibitions to network with other professionals and potentially sell your artwork. It can also be an avenue to find more clients.

- **Evaluate Income Streams** : Regularly assess which income streams are performing the best and which can be improved. Focus on scaling profitable areas.

# Ways of printing digital art

Printing digital art requires selecting the right method, materials, and services to ensure high-quality results that preserve the details and colors of your artwork. Here are various ways to print digital art:

1. **Giclée Printing**

- **Overview** : A popular, high-quality method for printing digital art, giclée printing uses inkjet printers with archival-quality inks on fine art paper or canvas.

- **Materials** : Archival papers (cotton rag, watercolor paper, etc.), canvas, or specialty papers.

- **Benefits** :
  - Vibrant, long-lasting colors.

  - Suitable for gallery-quality prints.

  - High-resolution detail preservation.

- **Best for** : Fine art prints, high-end photography, and artists looking to sell or exhibit prints.

- **Where to get it** : Professional print shops, online services like **Fine Art America** , **Printful** , or **CanvasPop** .

2. **Inkjet Printing**

- **Overview** : Using a high-resolution inkjet printer at home or a professional print shop can provide good-quality prints, especially if you have control over settings and materials.

- **Materials** : High-quality photo paper, matte or glossy paper, and archival ink.

- **Benefits** :
  - Accessible for home printing if you have a good printer.

  - Relatively inexpensive for small runs or test prints.

- **Best for** : Personal prints, limited runs, or smaller works.

- **Recommended Printers** : **Epson SureColor** or **Canon PIXMA Pro** series for high-quality home inkjet printing.

3. **Laser Printing**

- **Overview** : Laser printers use toner instead of ink and can produce sharp and vibrant images but may not match the detail and color depth of inkjet or giclée prints.

- **Materials** : Glossy or matte paper suitable for laser printing.

- **Benefits** :
  - Quick and cost-effective for bulk printing.

  - Great for posters, graphic designs, or large print runs.

- **Best for** : Posters, flyers, or digital art with less fine detail.

- **Where to get it** : Office supply stores, print shops, or through services like **Staples** or **FedEx Office** .

4. **Screen Printing**

- **Overview** : A technique where ink is pushed through a mesh screen onto the paper or fabric, often used for creating limited edition prints, apparel, or posters.

- **Materials** : Paper, fabric, or other materials suitable for screen printing.

- **Benefits** :
  - Great for bold, graphic artwork or illustrations.

  - Can be used for printing on textiles (T-shirts, bags, etc.).

- **Best for** : Limited edition prints, posters, or custom merchandise.

- **Where to get it** : Specialty print shops or through services like **Printful** or **Redbubble** .

5. **Dye Sublimation Printing**

- **Overview** : This process uses heat to transfer dye onto materials like fabric, metal, or ceramics. It's popular for creating prints on non-paper surfaces like T-shirts, mugs, or photo panels.

- **Materials** : Metal, ceramics, polyester fabric, photo panels, etc.

- **Benefits** :
  - Excellent for creating merchandise or photo prints on metal or fabric.

  - Durable and vibrant prints.

- **Best for** : Custom products like T-shirts, mugs, and posters on fabric or metal.

- **Where to get it** : Online services like **Zazzle** , **Shutterfly** , or **Society6** .

6. **Canvas Printing**

- **Overview** : A popular choice for digital art, this method involves printing your work directly onto canvas, which can then be stretched onto a frame.

- **Materials** : Canvas, often cotton or polyester-based.

- **Benefits** :
  - Gives a more textured, painterly effect.

  - Ready to hang without needing a frame.

- **Best for** : Digital paintings, photography, or large-format prints.

- **Where to get it** : Services like **CanvasPop** , **Mixtiles** , or **Snapfish** .

7. **Offset Printing**

- **Overview** : This method involves transferring ink from a plate to a rubber blanket and then onto the printing surface. Offset printing is ideal for mass production of high-quality prints.

- **Materials** : Glossy or matte paper, card stock, etc.

- **Benefits** :
  - Ideal for large-volume printing, reducing cost per print.

  - High-quality, consistent results.

- **Best for** : Posters, large print runs of digital art, or comics.

- **Where to get it** : Professional print houses, often used for publishing books or posters.

8. **Acrylic Prints**

- **Overview** : Digital artwork is printed onto high-quality photo paper, which is then sandwiched between sheets of acrylic, creating a glossy and modern look.

- **Materials** : Acrylic sheets and high-resolution photo paper.

- **Benefits** :
  - Vibrant colors with a glossy, professional finish.

  - Ideal for modern or minimalist decor.

- **Best for** : Large-format art prints or exhibitions.

- **Where to get it** : Specialty services like **WhiteWall** or **Printique** .

9. **Metal Prints**

- **Overview** : Artwork is printed directly onto a metal sheet, usually aluminum, which gives the art a unique, vibrant look with a glossy finish.

- **Materials** : Aluminum or other metal sheets.

- **Benefits** :
  - Durable and long-lasting.

  - Colors appear sharp and vibrant with a modern finish.

- **Best for** : Photography, digital art with bold colors, or art that will be displayed in modern spaces.

- **Where to get it** : Services like **Bay Photo** or **Shutterfly** .

10. **3D Printing (For Sculptural Digital Art)**

- **Overview** : For artists working in 3D design, you can print digital sculptures or models using a 3D printer with materials like plastic, resin, or even metal.

- **Materials** : Resin, PLA, ABS, or metal.

- **Benefits** :
  - Create physical models or sculptures from 3D digital files.

  - Wide range of materials and finishes.

- **Best for** : 3D artists, sculptors, or creating collectibles.

- **Where to get it** : Online services like **Shapeways** or **Sculpteo** .

#### Tips for High-Quality Prints:

1. **Resolution** : Ensure your artwork is at least 300 DPI (dots per inch) for printing. This ensures sharp, clear prints.

2. **Color Profile** : Use CMYK (Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, Black) color profiles when preparing files for printing, as this is the standard for most print processes.

3. **Paper Type** : Choose the paper type based on the finish you want:

- **Matte** : Less reflective, great for fine art prints.

- **Glossy** : High-shine, good for vibrant color reproduction.

- **Textured** : Adds a unique feel to your print, like watercolor paper or canvas.

By choosing the right printing method, you can ensure your digital art is presented in the best possible way, whether you're printing for personal use, sales, or exhibitions.

# Evangelion, Deep psychoanalysis

**Neon Genesis Evangelion** is perhaps the most celebrated and dissected anime when it comes to **deep psychoanalysis**, especially in the realms of **Freudian, Lacanian, and Jungian thought**. Its creator, **Hideaki Anno**, famously said he created _Evangelion_ to "understand himself." What emerged was a **psychological, philosophical, and symbolic descent** into the **psyche of a fractured subject**, mapped through apocalyptic science fiction.

Let’s explore _Evangelion_ through a **psychoanalytic lens**:

---

## 🧠 STRUCTURE: THE PSYCHIC LANDSCAPE

### ☢️ **Setting as Psyche**

- **Tokyo-3, NERV, and the Evangelions** represent **layers of the self**: external reality (ego), inner mechanisms (superego), and repressed desire (id).
- **The Angels** can be read as **invasive traumas** or repressed contents that must be “fought off” with borrowed structures (Evangelions = armored selves).

---

## 🧍 **Shinji Ikari – The Neurotic Subject**

| Element                                  | Psychoanalytic Interpretation                                           |
| ---------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Shinji’s phrase** “I mustn’t run away” | Repression mechanism, Ego under siege                                   |
| **Hedgehog’s dilemma**                   | Fear of intimacy vs. need for connection (Freud: ambivalence of desire) |
| **Entry plug synchronization**           | Identification with the maternal body (Eva = Mother)                    |
| **Fear of rejection, passive identity**  | Castration anxiety, symbolic lack (Lacan’s manque)                      |

Shinji is **neurotic**, constantly caught between **desire and inhibition**, unable to enter the symbolic fully—**he lacks the Name-of-the-Father**, and floats between drives and guilt.

---

## 👩‍👧 **Rei Ayanami – The Void**

- A clone, a vessel, a non-subject.
- Represents **the Real** in Lacan: the **unassimilable, pre-symbolic mother**.
- She is a **container of the mother’s body**, origin of LCL (primordial soup).
- Interactions with Rei are always **haunted by death** and **fascination with the origin**.

---

## 🔥 **Asuka Langley Soryu – The Imaginary Ego**

- Full of bluster, but dependent on recognition.
- Asuka is the **imaginary identification**, an armor of performance.
- Her collapse reflects the **shattering of the ego** when narcissistic supply fails.

> Her famous line: _"Look at me!"_ — a direct plea for symbolic confirmation of her existence.

---

## 🪞 **Misato Katsuragi – The Split Superego**

- Acts out sexually, drinks heavily, commands ruthlessly.
- She is **divided between a maternal role and a sexualized identity**, unable to reconcile them.
- Her relationship with Shinji mirrors the **confused transitional object**—too close to be safe, too distant to be nurturing.

---

## 🧬 **Instrumentality Project – The Fantasy of Wholeness**

- Gendo and SEELE attempt to **dissolve all human boundaries** and **return to a pre-Oedipal unity**, a **jouissance without lack**.
- This is the **Real without Symbolic mediation**—the trauma of undifferentiation.
- In psychoanalysis, this is **psychotic collapse** or **death drive in pure form**.

---

## 📺 **End of Evangelion (1997)** – Traversal of Fantasy

- Shinji is faced with the ultimate fantasy: a world without pain, without others.
- He chooses instead **to return to a world with separation, fear, and desire**.
- This is **Lacan’s traversal of fantasy**: he recognizes the impossibility of the Other's fullness and chooses **symbolic life with lack**.

> Final line: _"Kimochi warui."_ (“Disgusting”) — A jarring return to reality.

---

## 🔍 EVANGELION THROUGH LACAN’S RSI (Real, Symbolic, Imaginary)

| Lacanian Register | Character / Symbol                           |
| ----------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| **Imaginary**     | Asuka, Ego ideals, mecha combat glory        |
| **Symbolic**      | Misato, NERV hierarchy, language, scripts    |
| **Real**          | Rei, Angels, instrumentality, death, mothers |

---

## 🧾 CONCLUSION: WHY IT STILL MATTERS

_Evangelion_ is a **psychic crucible**. Instead of offering escape, it **mirrors back the audience’s own desire, fear, and pain**. It shows what happens when:

- The **Name-of-the-Father fails**
- The **symbolic collapses**
- The **Real invades** the ego

It doesn’t offer salvation—it offers **recognition**.

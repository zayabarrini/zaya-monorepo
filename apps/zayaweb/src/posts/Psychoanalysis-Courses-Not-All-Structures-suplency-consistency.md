2. The same solution, which consists of covering the thing with a fiction attached to an ideal signifier, works in many cases, but does not necessarily require the subject's delusional inventiveness.

### Loss and Refund

The neurotic strategy of inscribing jouissance, both in obsessional neurosis and in hysteria, would be marked by the equivalence between loss and restitution. The less jouissance , imposed by castration, would be made equivalent to an extra jouissance , understood by the phallic formations of restitution, as the symptom. The neurotic presupposition is that less and more are commensurable and reversible and that is why the lack in the Other is identified with the demand in the subject .

### **Maximum Enjoyment: The Marquis de Sade**

---

#### **📅 Chronology of a Life (1740-1768)**

- **1740** – Donatien-Alphonse-François, the Marquis de Sade, is born on June 2 in Paris.
- **1755** – Appointed ensign of infantry in the king's household.
- **1756** – The Seven Years' War begins. The young officer Sade receives several promotions and begins his first libertine actions.
- **1768** – **The Arcueil Scandal**: On April 3, he flogged a young beggar, Rose Keller, on Easter Sunday. She escaped and denounced him. He was imprisoned in Saumur on April 8, later transferred to Pierre-Enclise. On June 10, he was tried in Paris, sentenced to pay a fine, and released on November 16 by order of the king.

#### **⚰️ Legacy & Posthumous Influence (1814-2017)**

- **1814** – **December 2**: Sade dies of pulmonary edema. He was buried in a religious ceremony against his will, but his request for an anonymous grave was respected; the tomb has since been lost.
- **1904** – _The 120 Days of Sodom_ is published in Berlin by psychiatrist Iwan Bloch (under the pseudonym Eugen Dührer) in an edition containing many transcription errors.
- **1929** – Maurice Heine purchases the manuscript of _The 120 Days of Sodom_ in Berlin on behalf of the Viscount de Noailles.
- **1931-1935** – Maurice Heine publishes _The 120 Days of Sodom_ in France in three volumes.
- **1947** – Jean-Jacques Pauvert begins publishing the Complete Works of Sade; a decade later, he is sentenced to the confiscation and destruction of several of these works.
- **2014** – The bicentenary of Sade's death is listed as a national celebration in France. The Musée d’Orsey holds a major exhibition on his influence.
- **2017** – The French government declares the manuscript of _The 120 Days of Sodom_ a "national treasure."
